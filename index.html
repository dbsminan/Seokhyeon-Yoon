About

What I focus on and why.

What I do
Design · Fabrication · Measurement
End-to-End

I develop wafer-scale SiN photonic structures on glass substrates and experimentally validate their optical performance. A key challenge in i-line stepper lithography is unavoidable stitching error; my work investigates taper-based structures to mitigate stitching-induced loss while preserving manufacturability.

Why it matters
Scalable system integration
Impact

As packaging-scale integration expands to panel and wafer levels, routing length and loss become primary bottlenecks. Low-loss, wafer-scale optical interconnects can enable higher bandwidth density and energy efficiency in future CPO systems.

Selected focus areas.

Mode matching via taper engineering
CPO

Designed coupling structures between Si and SiN waveguides for efficient PIC–EIC optical interfaces. Optimized taper geometry to reduce coupling loss and improve robustness to fabrication constraints.

M.S.: Wafer-Level Optical Interconnect (SiN on Glass)
Low-loss stitching structures with taper-assisted design
Main Track

Fabricate SiN waveguides on glass and quantify stitching-induced loss using dedicated test structures. Develop taper-assisted stitching designs to improve wafer-scale routing performance with practical lithography.

  • Tools: ANSYS Lumerical FDTD / MODE / EME
  • Validation: chip-scale verification and wafer-scale optical measurement
